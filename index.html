Where in the world?

A little bit about me

Hi, I'm Kate, I grew up in Devon(the most beautiful county in the UK) I recently made the move to Bristol to work and study because unfortunately we can't live at home rent-free forever! I took 2018-2019 as a gap year and it finally allowed me to fulfil my dream to travel on my own accord. My plan is to continue that dream, but this time fitting it around a busy schedule of work and study.

What I'm doing now

Right now, I am on the first year of a four-year Engineering Degree Apprenticeship. I have 5 weeks of holiday per year and have a less-than-average salary. My passion is exploring the world and while I have less of an oppurtunity to do that at this moment in time, I want to share my travel experiences and tips with you to to ensure you have the best trip possible!.

COLOMBIA

Colombia is the second most biodiverse country in the world and I was lucky enough to see this incredible country from a local's perspective. This place has it all: the Andes, the Amazon, deserts, beaches and everything inbetween and it's safe to say that it is the best country I have visited by a long shot. My trip was planned to be three weeks long(emphasis on 'planned' here because unfortunately things rarely go to plan). Our route began in Bogota and covered the cities and pueblos of(in order) Cali-Pance-Salento-Manizales-San Andres Islands-Mingueo and Tayrona National Park-Valledupar Cabo de la Vela-Cartagena- San Luis.
